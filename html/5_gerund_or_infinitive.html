Gerund or Infinitive


The GERUND is often used for an action that happens before or at the same time as the action of the main verb
The formula is "verb + gerund". Examples:

1. I enjoy playing --- 2. I denied stealing


The INFINITIVE is often used for actions that follow the action of the main verb.
The formula is "verb + infinitive". Examples:

1. I decided to visit my uncle --- 2. I want to go out


A verb can be followed by another verb. The second usually needs to change into the "-ing" form or the "to + infinitive"
form. Wich form you needs depend on what the first verb is. There's many rules:


I - Verbs followed by the -ING form
When enjoy, admit and mind are followed by another verb, it must be in -ing.

*I enjoy travelling --- *He admited stealing the necklace --- *I don't mind waiting if you're busy


II - After verbs that express likes/dislikes
Like, love, enjoy, dislike, hate
*I like playing soccer but I hate boxing.
NOTE: "like, love, hate,..." can also be followed by an infinitive
*I like to watch TV in the evening.

III - After verbs such as:
admit --> He admitted stealing the wallet
advise --> I wouldn't advise buying a used car
allow --> They don't allow smoking here -- NOTE: When an object is introduced an infinitive is used instead of gerund.
anticipate --> He anticipated passing the exam
avoid --> She avoided meeting him
appreciate --> I appreciate working with you
complete --> They completed building their house
consider --> She considered starting a new business
delay --> She delayed doing her homework
deny --> He deneied stealing the money
fancy --> Fancy meeting you here!
finish --> She finished writting the letter
go --> Go swimming
imagine --> He imagines meeting her
involve --> The job involves working in teams
keep --> He keeps complaining about his grilfriend
mention --> He mentioned working in that company
mind --> He mentioned working in that company
miss --> He misses talking to her
permit --> They don't permit smoking here. -- SAME NOTE AS "ALLOW"
postpone --> they postponed traveling to Japan
practice --> She practiced painting
reject --> He rejected working with them
risk --> She risks losing her job
suggest --> I suggest leaving early
resist --> He couldn't resist eating the cake
waste time/money --> Don't waste my time complaining

IV - After prepositions: keep on, interesed in, instead of, good at, before, after, etc.
*I'm interesed in collecting stamps --- *After playing football i drank orange juice


V - After some expressions: It's no use..., It's not good..., There's no point in..., I can't help..., I don't mind..., I can't stand..., etc.
*It's no use convicing him to met her
