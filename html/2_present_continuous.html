Present Continuous

We use the present continuous in the next cases:

1. Describe actions that occurr at the same time they are counted or written
*You are studying English

2. Establish contexts, talk about general, personal or social situations that occur currently
*She is working a lot lately

3. Advance facts, quotes, events or actions that will surely occur in the future
*They are going to the zoo next Saturday

4. Mention temporary events
*Today is snowing, but yesterday was sunny

5. Describe actions that occur constantly.
*The birds are always singing since sunrise


The structure for this tense is the following:

+ = "subject + verb to be + veb + -ing + complement"

I am listening to music
The elephant is playing in the water

- = "subject + verb to be + not + verb + -ing + complement"

I am not listening to music
The elephant isn't playing in the water

? = "verb to be + subject + verb + -ing + complement + ?"

Am I listening to music?
Is the elephant playing in the water?
