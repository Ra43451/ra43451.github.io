Simple Present

We use the present simple in the next cases:

1. For simple for repeated or regular actions in the present time period
*I take the train to the office

2. For facts
*A dog has four legs

3. For habits
*I get up early every day

4. For things that are always generally true
*It rains a lot in winter


The structure for this tense is the following:

+ = "subject + verb + complement"

I am a teacher
We eat pizza

- = "subject + verb + not + complement"

I am not a teacher
You don't eat pizza

? = "verb + subject + complement + ?"

Are we friends?
Does he eat pizza?
