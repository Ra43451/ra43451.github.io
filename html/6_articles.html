Gerund or Infinitive


The definite article (THE) is used before a noun to indicate that the identity if the noun is known to the reader

The indefinite article (A/AN) is used before a noun that is general or when its identity is not known

There are certain situations in wich a noun takes no article

I - Specific identity not known
Use the indefinite article "a" or "an" only with a singular count noun whose specific identity is not known to the reader.
Use "a" before nouns that begins with a consonant sound, and use "an" before nouns that begin with a vowel sound.

Use the artcile "a" or "an" to indicate any non-specified member of a group or category.
*I think an animal is in the garage --- *That man is a scoundrel

Use the article "a" or "an" to indicate one in number (as opposed to more than one).
*I own a cat and two dogs

Use the article "a" before a consonant, and use "an" before a vowel sound.
*A boy --- *An apple

Sometimes an adjective comes between the article and noun: an unhappy boy, a red apple, etc.
The plural form for "a" or "an" is SOME. Use "some" to indicate an unspecified, limited amount (but more than one).
*an apple --- *some apples


II - Specific identity known
Use the definite article "the" with any noun (whether singular or plural,count or non-count) when the specific identity of the noun is known to the reader.
*I like playing soccer but I hate boxing.

Use the article "the" when a particular noun has already been mentioned previously
*I ate an apple yesterday. The apple was juicy and delicious.

Use the article "the" when an objective phrase or clause describing the noun clarifies or restrict it's identity
*The boy next to me raised his hands.

Use the article "the" when the noun refers to something or someone that is unique
*The theory of relativity.


III - All things or things in general
Use "no article" with plural count nouns or any non-count nouns used to mean all or in general.
*Threes are beautiful in the fall (All threes are beautiful in the fall)
*He was asking for advice (He was asking for advice in general)
*I don't like coffee (I don't like coffee in general)
