Reported Speech

Reported Speech is used when we tell someone what another person said. To do this we can use Direct or Indirect Speech.

Direct speech is used to repeat what someone else has said, and indirect speech is used to report on what someone else has said.

Pronouns also usually changes in indirect speech

"I enjoy working in my garden", said Bob --> Bob said that he enjoyed working in his garden.
"We played tennis for our school", said Alina --> Alina told me they'd played tennis for thei school.


However, if you are the person or one of the people who spoke, the pronouns don't change

"I'm working on my thesis", I said --> I told her that I was working on my thesis.
"We wanted our jobs back", we said --> We said that we wanted our jobs back.


We also change demonstratives and adverbs of time and place if they are no longer accurate.

"This is my house" --> He said this was his house. (You are currently in front of the house.)
"This is my house" --> He said that was his house. (You aren't currently in front of the house)


In the same way these changes to THOSE, now changes to THEN, yesterday changes to THE DAY BEFORE,

tomorrow changes to THE NEXT/THE FOLLOWING DAY and ago changes to BEFORE.
