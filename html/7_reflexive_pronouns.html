Reflexive Pronouns


   Reflexive pronouns are words like myself, yourself, himself, etc.They refer back to a person or thing.
We often use reflexive pronouns when the subject and the object of a verb are the same.
*I cut myself when I was making dinner last night


I - Adding Emphasis
1. Add a reflexive pronoun for emphasis when it's unusual or different
*He wants to pass his driving test so that he can drive himself to work.
2. Use reflexive pronouns to emphasise that someone does it personally, not anybody else.
*The door was definitely locked. I locked it myself.
3. Use a reflexive pronoun together with the noun it refers to in order to emphasise it.
*We talked to the manager herself, and she agreed to give us our money back.


II - By + Reflexive pronoun
Use it, to mean alone.
*He usually goes on holiday by himself.


III - Reciprocal Pronouns
Notice the difference between plural reflexive pronouns and reciprocal pronouns (each other, one other). With reciprocal
pronouns (e.g. each other), each person does the action to the other person/people but not themselves.
*They're buying themselves a new television
*They're buying each other small
